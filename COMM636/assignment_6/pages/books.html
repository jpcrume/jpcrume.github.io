Coming Soon: Sunrise on the Reaping

Coming March 18, 2025

Step back into the world of Panem, twenty-four years before the events of The Hunger Games. Sunrise on the Reaping explores the morning of the reaping for the Fiftieth Hunger Games, also known as the Second Quarter Quell. This new chapter delves into themes of propaganda, power, and the ease with which the many are governed by the few. Inspired by David Hume's reflections on submission and control, Suzanne Collins crafts a thought-provoking narrative that challenges readers to confront the question: Real or not real?

The Hunger Games Series

Enter the dystopian world of Panem, where Katniss Everdeen’s act of bravery sparks a rebellion against the Capitol’s cruel Hunger Games. As she battles for survival and freedom, Katniss becomes the face of a revolution that challenges the oppressive rule of President Snow. From the deadly arenas to the origins of the Games, this series explores courage, sacrifice, and the fight for a better future.

Discover the stories that have captivated readers worldwide, from Katniss's journey in The Hunger Games, Catching Fire, and Mockingjay, to the chilling origins of the Games in The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes. Experience the newly released The Hunger Games: Illustrated Edition and look forward to the upcoming Sunrise on the Reaping, set during the 50th Hunger Games.

The Underland Chronicles

Follow Gregor and his sister, Boots, as they tumble into the Underland, a hidden world beneath New York City teeming with giant creatures and dangerous prophecies. Tasked with fulfilling destinies he never asked for, Gregor must battle monstrous rats, cure deadly plagues, and uncover dark secrets threatening the fragile peace.

With courage and sacrifice at its heart, this epic series builds to a thrilling finale where the fate of the Underland—and Gregor’s own family—hangs in the balance.

Standalone Books

From heartfelt stories to thrilling mysteries, Suzanne Collins’ standalone books showcase her remarkable range as a storyteller. Explore the playful charm of When Charlie McButton Lost Power, a delightful children’s tale about imagination and connection, or delve into the poignant and personal reflections of Year of the Jungle, inspired by her own childhood.

Collins also brings her creative genius to unique projects like Fire Proof in The Mystery Files of Shelby Woo, combining intrigue and adventure, and Skinny Dipping, a sharp, satirical novel. Each standalone work offers a fresh perspective, demonstrating her versatility and depth across genres and audiences.
