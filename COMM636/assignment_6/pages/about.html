About Suzanne Collins

Suzanne Collins is a celebrated author known for her defining works that captivate readers of all ages. Best known for The Hunger Games series, she has left an undeniable mark on modern literature, combining gripping storytelling with profound social commentary.


Her earlier works include The Underland Chronicles, a fantastical adventure series exploring destiny and bravery, and several standalone titles that demonstrate her versatility, including the autobiographical Year of the Jungle and the playful When Charlie McButton Lost Power.

Fun Facts About Suzanne

  • She began her career writing for children's television, working on shows like Clarissa Explains It All and Little Bear.
  • The idea for The Hunger Games came to her while channel surfing between reality TV and war coverage.
  • Her works have been translated into over 50 languages and sold millions of copies worldwide.
